Project Details

Everyday Speech is a company that creates videos and worksheets to assist speech therapy instructors and interested parents. They came to Scout, Northeasternâ€™s design studio, looking for an identity and website redesign. Our team of three designers and two developers set out to first tackle the identity redesign, then look at the website structure and design.

Logos

Our design team drew inspiration from images of speech and technology for our logo ideas. We ended up going with the icon featuring a set of quotes within a play button.

Website Layout & Styling

After deciding on a logo direction, our team moved on to the website redesign. We began by looking at the content of the old website and organizing it based on user stories that we came up with. We reorganized the navigation and page structure so that the content was organized by videos and worksheets and the login for paying subscribers was always available. For visualizing these first ideas, we created wireframes and then moved onto visual design. The process began with simple typography and colors, and ended with patterns and gradients.

Design for Wireframes

At the beginning of the design process, we created a basic type and color template. As we continued designing pages, we used this to build off of by adding patterns and other visual elements.

Illustrations and Style Guide

At one point in our design process, we had the structure complete and basic styling in place, but needed an extra push for design. We decided to integrate illustrations into the pages, both through explanatory elements and subtle, colored patterns. These give the final website a friendly feel that teachers can connect with. Our project concluded with the creation of a style guide that shows all of the elements of the Everyday Speech identity and how they work together.
